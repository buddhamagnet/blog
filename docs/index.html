Into the woods

As I'm about to embark on a two year journey into the woods and beyond with the marvellous people at Woodland Ways, I thought I'd kick off a blog to document the sights and sounds of that mission. And any mountains I visit in the meantime. Or countries. And talk about some of the kit I use. With a side order of tech and radical politics and music.

Into the woods

2017-02-20 09:23:48 +0000 UTC

So I start a two year journey in the woods near Appleton, Oxfordshire this weekend with Woodland Ways. Friday to Sunday in February. Needed a large pack so I could take everything I needed, and settled on the Atmos 65 from Osprey. Amazing piece of kit. Right now it's holding my tent, four season sleeping bag, cooking kit, axe and knife, clothes and food and the only item outside is the self-inflating mat. The guy who reviews it below doesn't mention the built-in rain cover, but I'm more likely to need it than him.
As for blades, haven't decided on a new knife as yet, but I've packed a wildlife hatchet from Gransfors Bruk. I've had it for a few years and it's never let me down. Also the perfect size to take into the woods for a weekend.
I tend to sleep on the ground in the colder months, and go up into a hammock as it gets warmer, so for this trip I'll be bedding down in the Snugpak Ionosphere. Goes up in a few minutes and surprisingly weather-proof.
I try and pack everthing I need and no more, but it's February in the UK so my clothes comprise a Merino base layer and long johns, some trousers by Fjallraven, and some waterpoof trousers and fleece, both by Keela. So I should be warm and dry. Now, to coat all this with the sweet smell of woodsmoke!
